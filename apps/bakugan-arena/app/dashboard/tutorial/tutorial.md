# Bakugan Arena – Official Tutorial

## 1. Game Overview

**Bakugan Arena** is a strategic turn-based battle game where players deploy Bakugans, use Ability Cards, and control Gate Cards to defeat their opponent.

The objective is simple: **eliminate all enemy Bakugans or outlast your opponent through strategic play.**

---

## 2. Core Rules

Each player starts the game with:

- **3 Bakugans**
- **3 Ability Cards (maximum usable per game)**
- **3 Gate Cards (maximum usable per game)**

---

## 3. Victory Conditions

A player wins the game if:

- All **3 opponent Bakugans are eliminated**
- The **opponent’s timer reaches 0** *(only after Turn 0 conditions are no longer active)*

---

## 4. Draw Conditions

A match ends in a draw if:

- No more Gate Cards can be placed by either player
- All Bakugans from both players are eliminated in the same turn
- A player’s timer reaches 0 during **Turn 0**

---

## 5. Battle System

A battle begins when **two opposing Bakugans land on the same Gate Card**.

Each battle lasts **2 turns**:

### Turn 1
- The player who first occupied the Gate Card acts first
- They may:
  - Use an Ability Card
  - Activate the Gate Card (if they own it, and it is neither already activated nor blocked)

### Turn 2
- The opponent takes their turn with the same options

### End of Battle Resolution
After both turns:

- The Gate Card effect is resolved (if applicable)
- The Bakugan with the **lowest power level is eliminated**

---

## 6. Ability Cards System

Ability Cards are automatically filtered by the game before being added to a player’s hand.

If a card cannot be used in the current context, it is **not given to the player**.

### Examples

- **Air Battle**
  - Allows movement to another Gate Card
  - If no other Gate Cards exist, the card is not added to the hand

- **Marionette**
  - Allows control/movement of enemy Bakugans
  - If no valid target exists (e.g., only one Bakugan remains on the field), the card is not added

---

## 7. Neutral Phase (No Active Battle)

When no battle is currently occurring, players can perform **one action per turn**.

### Available Actions

#### 1. Place a Gate Card
- First possible action
- Does not restrict other future actions during the turn

#### 2. Deploy a Bakugan
- Second possible action
- If performed, the player can no longer place a Gate Card this turn

#### 3. Use an Ability Card
- Third possible action
- Immediately ends the turn after use
- Prevents any other action this turn

---

## 8. Strategic Note

Efficient play in Bakugan Arena is about **resource timing and board control**:

- Gate placement defines battle opportunities
- Ability Cards can shift momentum but are limited
- Overextending early often leads to tactical disadvantage

Mastering the interaction between positioning and ability usage is key to victory.
